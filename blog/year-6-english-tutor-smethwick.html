Primary and 11+ Progress Approach

High-impact primary tuition combines reading fluency, reasoning practice, and careful feedback. For 11+ preparation, students benefit from regular verbal, non-verbal, maths, and comprehension practice with clear pacing.

What Sessions Should Cover

  • Warm-up retrieval for spellings, number facts or reasoning patterns
  • One focused teaching objective per lesson
  • Guided practice with immediate corrections
  • Small home tasks to keep momentum between sessions

Parent Checklist for the First Month

  1. Set one literacy and one maths target.
  2. Track homework completion and confidence weekly.
  3. Review mock-style questions at the end of week 4.
  4. Adjust the plan before moving to the next set of topics.

Example 4-Week English Plan

  • Week 1: reading comprehension basics and inference question structure
  • Week 2: grammar focus (punctuation, clause control, sentence accuracy)
  • Week 3: timed writing task with feedback on structure and vocabulary
  • Week 4: SATs-style mixed paper and parent progress summary

Common Year 6 English Problems and Fixes

Problem: answers are too short in comprehension. Fix: teach a “point + quote + explain” answer frame.

Problem: weak punctuation in writing. Fix: daily short editing drills with immediate correction.

Problem: low confidence before tests. Fix: regular timed mini-practice so format becomes familiar.

Parent FAQs

How often should Year 6 English tutoring run? One focused session weekly plus two short home tasks is usually enough for steady progress.

When should SATs-focused support start? Ideally at least one term before SATs to build reading speed and writing consistency without cramming.

Can tutoring help if my child dislikes English? Yes, when sessions are short, structured, and linked to quick wins each week.
